Start Here

What this actually is

The whole model in 15 seconds.

Three moving parts:

  1. You find small jobs on Upwork and send proposals.
  2. Claude builds the work — scripts, pages, spreadsheets, docs.
  3. You review it, deliver it, get paid and reviewed.

Your 2–5 hrs/week go only to the human parts: searching, submitting, and the final quality check. Claude does the heavy lifting.

Start Here

5 rules that keep you alive

Break one and you can lose the account before earning a dollar.

1Never scrape or auto-submit on the platform. You paste jobs to Claude by hand.
2Be honest about AI. If a job says "no AI," skip it.
3No client logins. You deliver files, never take their passwords.
4You review everything before it goes out. "The AI did it" is never an excuse.
5No client names or dollar amounts in this repo. It's a public page. Private data stays in the tracker on your device.

Each week · Find & win (~1 hr)

Copy 10–20 listings

Volume in, so Claude can hand you only the winners.

Run your 4 saved searches. Copy the full text (title, description, budget, client stats) of every plausible job into one block.

Good sign: clear scope, payment verified, has hired before. Skip: vague "got an idea, let's chat," no budget, brand-new client with nothing verified.

On a win · Deliver (~30 min)

Test it yourself

You are the quality gate. Every 5-star review depends on this.

Run the deliverable against the acceptance checklist Claude gives you. Does it work on their real data? Match the agreed format? Handle the edge cases they named?

If anything's off, send it back to Claude before it ever reaches the client.

On a win · Deliver (~30 min)

Deliver & ask for the review

Reviews are the asset. Speed + a clean handoff earns them.

Send the files + the short how-it-works note. Confirm they're happy, then politely ask for a review.

Move the tracker card to Delivered, then Paid.

Aim for 24–48 hr turnaround. Speed is the one edge you have over established freelancers.

Level up

The 3 pricing phases

Charge to win first, charge properly later.

1Jobs 1–5 — farm reviews. Bid $30–150, priced to win. Goal is five 5-star reviews, not profit.
2After 5 reviews — market rate. $50–400 depending on category. Raise ~20% every 5 jobs while you keep winning.
3After ~15 jobs — productize. Open a Fiverr storefront for whatever converts best.

Never bid below $30 — that segment is all headache.

Level up

Honest expectations

So you measure success against reality, not hype.

Month 1: ~$100–400 total. You're buying a sellable account with real reviews. That's the win.

Months 2–3: at market rate and ~3 wins/week, roughly $300–900/month inside your 2–5 hrs.

Anyone promising more, faster, on a zero-review account is selling something. The compounding asset is your review history plus a tracker full of data on what you actually win.
